So, I have had a long held interest in Artificial Intelligence. However, due to my own inadequacy and laziness, I never really got very far into implementing much of anything that could be in the realm of artificial intelligence. But, I wish to begin some more now with Genetic Algorithms.

Initial Concept

I originally thought of this idea as I was reading into genetic algorithms on this page about a year ago. This page put it into very understandable terms and allowed me to begin thinking about where this principle could be applied.

This was also around the time that I started playing a game called Stardew Valley.

This game is a farm life simulator with Zelda-like elements etc. I will let you look into reviews of the game if you are interested. What is more pertinent is the farming part of this game.

Sprinklers

In the game, you are to plant crops and water them daily in order to make money. Watering them every day takes energy out of your character, and energy is an important resource. Luckily, there are these items called sprinklers:

As shown in the image, there are three types of sprinklers. They increase in cost of production rather drastically. So, the problem I encountered was that, in the early game, I was able to get a lot of the most basic sprinkler (the left most one). However, it has a less clean pattern (for a tile based game) than the other two. So, it was not immediately obvious to me what would be the best way to tile these in an optimal way given that they would need to fit into a space where a scarecrow can protect them (keeps crops from being destroyed). The radius and shape of a scarecrow looks like this:

Now, this problem can be easily brute forced with some minor trial and error and good old pen and paper. However, I began wondering if I could write an algorithm to find the optimal layout. Then I realized that a genetic algorithm might work rather nicely here.

Time to do a Side Project!

Fast forward to today. So, ever since college began, I have been lamenting that I have not had enough time to do my own programming. So, I decided that over my winter break I would take this concept and bring it to life. I hope to be able to write a genetic algorithm that can solve this problem of finding the optimal layout of sprinklers in order to fit the range of a single scarecrow.

Some details that I have already thought of:

Fitness Function

Can be defined as initially just the total number of tiles that are watered in the area of the scarecrow. This gives us a very simple and quantifiable metric to judge "chromosomes"/"organisms" in the algorithm.

I may later want to extend the fitness function to check whether the entire space can be traversed. This is important because in the game, if I cannot traverse to it, the tile would be useless.

I may also later extend this to check if the design is tilable and prioritize designs that can be tiled.

Encoding and Decoding

Encoding and decoding for now will simply be to an array of 1s and 0s (maybe bits of an int even I'm not sure yet). A 1 will denote a sprinkler being there while a 0 will denote no sprinkler.

UI

After the core functionality is there, I hope to add a UI to this program in order to visualize the best species at any point in time. The UI will also allow monitoring and data feedback. I also hope to include a graph in there and a place to change configuration values of the algorithm.

Final Words

Overall, I think it will be a fun little project. I can make good use of it. Most of all, I believe the scope is doable (perhaps not the UI, we will see) for the duration of my winter break.

Well, I will keep updates on the progress of it, but I hope this becomes a fruitful project.

Realization

I realized that I am slowly losing sight of the reason that I began to write this language in the first place. Well ok, there are admittedly two reasons:

  1. I wanted to learn how compilers work.
  2. I wanted a replacement for a scripting language in my workflow that was entirely my own.

Wow it's been a while.

So, my first (and last) update on this was... 4 months ago??? Wow... Well now that it is summer time, I have more time to update this blog. The progress on the Toy Language didn't just not progress at all during all that time, I have been working on it in my spare time in between school. However, now I have more time to dedicate on it.

Progress

During the past four months, I have been focusing most of my time mostly coming up with the lexical analyzer and the parser. However, now I am realizing that I have delved so deep into my code that I lost sight of the bigger picture. So, now I am taking a step back and re-evaluating the steps I need to take.

VM

The first step I would like to take now is to step away from my compiler code and make a Virtual Machine. I have been doing some research on virtual machines during the past week and I have learned quite a bit. I have two basic implementations of a stack machine and a register machine. While my knowledge is imperfect, from what I can understand, these are the perks and downsides of the two systems:

Stack Machine

Positives

  • Highly portable
  • Operands are implicit
  • Easy to write compilers for

Negatives

  • More instructions (PUSH and POP ops)
  • Relatively slower than register machine
  • Difficult to optimize Bytecode

Register Machine

Positives

  • Fewer instructions
  • Easy to optimize Bytecode
  • Relatively faster

Negatives

  • Not as portable
  • Long instructions
  • Difficult to write compilers for

What I decided to use

For the given reasons, while the register machine was very tempting, I have decided to use the stack machine due to the relative ease of creating one. There is nothing to stop me from switching to a register machine later on. But for now, for simplicity's sake, I am going to be going forward with a stack machine.

Conclusion

So, for now, that's all. I will probably make more regular updates on the progress of the VM. As soon as the VM is done, I will be able to move forward and relook at my compiler. Hopefully, with a VM that I can understand, I will have an easier time parsing and converting the language into the necessary format.
