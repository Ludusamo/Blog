Project: Work Hard, Play Hard

05 June 2016 on Programming, Project, Pebble, Time, Round, Pebble Time Round, Work Hard Play Hard

JUNE 5, 2016

Inspiration

I am in my senior year of high school. If you are in the states, you will know how quickly motivation for seniors in high school to do work deteriorates. As such, I was finding that I was spending an increasing amount of time playing games and not doing more "worthwhile" things.

One of these games that I played was Guild Wars 2. I picked up the game over the winter holidays to play with my friends, and I binged this game HARD. It was clearly becoming an issue. However, regardless of that, one feature that I really liked about this game was that it had a simple command called: /age. Essentially what this did, was just tell you how much time total you have spent playing the game.

This got me thinking, I should really start tracking the time I spend working and playing. Around that time, I got a Pebble Time Round, so I decided to get off my butt and make an application.

Idea

So, the application would essentially do exactly what I described. On the simplest level, I want the application to be able to tell me the distribution of time that I have spent "playing" as opposed to "working".

I already have a preliminary version of the application up. Here are a couple pictures:

Select between modes.
Start Time.
Stop Time.
I even have a pretty nice first visualizer in there.

Future

Right now, the app is very basic. It only keeps track of the total amount of time "playing" or "working" in a single day. I might release a preliminary version with just this. However, I plan to expand it to store data over extended periods of time (companion application or online application?). But that will take a little bit of time. I also hope to tag this information, so I might even be able to see the distribution of time that I spend on individual things.

But for now, I am pretty proud that I finally got off my butt to do something that looks like it might actually reach completion.

Well, until next time.
