Refocusing my Language

24 September 2016 on Programming, Open Source, Project, C, Programming Language, Compiler

Toy Language Update

I recently released version 0.17.0 of my Toy Language. The release occurred right before school began (it is my first year of college), so I decided to take a little break from it.

During these past few weeks, while I did some bug fixing and testing, development has been slow. This gave me a lot of time to think. Think about what direction this language is headed.

Realization

I realized that I am slowly losing sight of the reason that I began to write this language in the first place. Well ok, there are admittedly two reasons:

  1. I wanted to learn how compilers work.
  2. I wanted a replacement for a scripting language in my workflow that was entirely my own.

I want to concentrate on that second point. I am losing sight of the fact that I want to make a scripting language.

I realized that I am spending far too much time pondering design decisions that are far too out of scope for what I had originally set out to do. I am trying to create a language that fits more functions than it was originally conceptualized for. And thats bad. My scope is TOO DAMN BIG.

Time to Reign it In

Going forward I have decided on a few things in no particular order:

  1. This language is a scripting language. Whether it can be a standalone or not will just be a nice side effect.
  2. The primary goal of this scripting language is to be small and fast.
  3. Everything will be an Object. I spent too much time trying to separate values, primitives, and objects. No more. Everything will be simpler and modular if I just make everything an object.

Conclusion

While substancial progress has been made (in fact I am really proud of my progress), I kind of let this project go a little too wild. I need to decide on what I want to do, keep it small, and complete it. Then I can add on to it. Lessons that I clearly did not learn from.

Anyway, that's all for now.
